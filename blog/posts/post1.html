The Identity Crisis

I was born 19 years ago in chilly November, ever since then, I have never been stationary in one place. In the span of 19 years, I have lived in 8 different locations which I can at least recall. Averaging about 2.3 years for a single location, the shiftings were difficult and tiresome, loading our whole house on a truck every 3 or so years were not easy but with time I got accustomed to this ritual.

Leaving a place and knowing I won’t be coming here ever again, and losing all my friends which I made only a year back was a depressing feeling which was only to be followed by a completely new place, new people and a new school, this was a brutal ordeal for a ten-year-old me knowing that this is going to be repeated again after few years.

Now comes the identity crisis part, whenever people asks me “where are you from?” My mind standstill for instant thinking what should be the answer to this question which is quite puzzling for me. Where is home? Is the address on my address proof my home? Even if I didn’t ever live there, is the place where I earned my first school certificate was my home or is it the place where I first went to school was my home. Is the place where I lived longest is my home or is it the place where my parents were born could be my home. It continues and as the ever-growing confusion.

"“Home is not where you are, but who you are”

While trying to placate with my ever varying addresses I realized that home is not where you are but who you are. Let’s see what the future holds for me and is there going to be any place which I can truly call “my home”?
