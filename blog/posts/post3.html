Lysergic Acid Diethylamide-25

Segregating your entire life to determine the best parts of it is hardly feasible because each moment of our lives has its own significance but one of the most vivid memories of my life is experiencing the powers of Lysergic Acid Diethylamide-25 or LSD for short.

LSD is a powerful psychedelic drug which can induce an altered state of consciousness, it sort of unlocks dimensions which are invisible to us in a normal state of consciousness. Small details become significant, the world feels fluid, colors appear more vibrant and thoughts become deep and sense full. And yes higher dosage can produce hallucination, which makes one sense and see things which are not present physically.

How does it Work?

Synthesized in 1938 from a rye fungus, ergot it was initially used to reduce uterine contractions during childbirth but there was no positive impact hence research was dropped. In 1943 Swiss chemist Albert Hoffman synthesized LSD to experiment but fortunately, somehow he got some of it on his fingers and unknowingly ingested small dosage of the powerful hallucinogen. As the result, he started feeling weird shortly after it (it takes few hours to metabolize) and decided to ride to his home on a bicycle while he was unknowingly tripping on LSD, and this is now celebrated as “Bicycle Day”.

The Elixer

When ingested LSD is metabolized by the liver and this takes around an hour after which the effects of the drug onsets. LSD acts as 5-HT or Serotonin receptor activator which means that it temporarily disables the system which regulates the serotonin levels in the body. Serotonin is a complex neurotransmitter which is associated with feelings of well-being, happiness, reward, memory, learning, and several other psychological functions.

This drastic change in serotonin levels alters the working of Pre-Frontal Cortex of brain which is involved in personality, social behavior, decision making and planning complex cognitive actions such as thoughts and feelings.

Recent scientific studies also show that LSD reduces blood flow to control centers of the brain which dampens their activity giving rise to creative thoughts, hallucinations, and feeling of ‘Ego-Death’.

My Experience

Saying taking LSD transformed me in a positive way may not be far-fetched or over-rated. I really can’t explain accurately due to my limited vocabulary and maybe also due to the shallowness of our languages. But I felt the cool breeze rising from the ocean, saltiness in the air, particles of sand, the crackling of wood, ever glowing flame and energy radiated from fire, calmness of lake waters along with the fear of roaring waves rising in the ocean on moonlit night and finally feeling of oneness with the universe.

"World would have been better place if everyone did psychedelics once in their life"
